CAREERS

ABA Therapy and BCBA Career Opportunities

in Arizona, Phoenix, Peoria, Scottsdale, Tempe, Gilbert and Chandler

At Advanced Autism Services, we are committed to hiring passionate, qualified therapists and BCBAs to join our team and provide the highest level of in-home care to our clients in the Phoenix and Scottsdale area(s). We pride ourselves in offering a career-oriented work environment where our team members can grow and learn in their field. ABA Therapy and BCBA roles offer rewarding work that improves the lives of children living with Autism Spectrum Disorder. ABA Therapy and BCBA staff can choose from full- time or part-time work, making this opportunity perfect for those seeking flexible work hours. If you are a qualified ABA Therapy professional or a BCBA in the Phoenix or Scottsdale area looking to take your career to the next level, we encourage you to reach out to us and apply to one of our current open positions!

Clinical Psychologist

What You'll Do:

  • Develop proficiency with diagnostic procedures.
  • Conduct standardized interviews with parents as well as communicate results and provide clinical guidance and recommendations for treatment.
  • Prepare reports for parents, participating pediatricians, and medical records.
  • Participate in weekly department meetings and/or ongoing training.
  • Additional responsibilities based on experience and available opportunities, including research and clinical supervision.

Who You Are:

  • Doctoral degree in Clinical Psychology with some experience administering and scoring psychological tests and a desire to work with children.
  • Clinical assessment and/or treatment experience with individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder is preferred, but we will consider appropriately motivated candidates.
  • Must be clearly on track for Arizona Clinical Licensure (have license within 3 months of hire).
  • Demonstrated ability to interview, perform psychological assessments, and provide a diagnosis based on test scores and observation.
  • Experience preparing reports for parents, medical personnel, and medical records.
  • Ability to effectively engage in one to one communication with parents and clients of all ages.

Clinical Psychologist

Job Type: Internship

Responsibilities:

  • Administer neuropsychological evaluation.
  • Report writing.
  • Experience in cognitive remediation.

Requirements:

  • PSYD or PHD doctorate clinical psychology program from APA accredited Doctoral Program in Clinical Psychology, Neuropsychology, Counseling, or Health.
  • Experience administrating psychology or neuropsychology assessment. Have experience with either child, adolescent and adult assessment, experience with traumatic brain injury
  • Experience in report writing but not required.
